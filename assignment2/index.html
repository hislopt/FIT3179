Sheep Around The World

This partitioned poster looks at sheep density around the world, and at New Zealand in particular, for its significant sheep population compared to its human one.

Global Sheep Density (2018)

Please mouse-over to see how many sheep there is spread over each nation in the world, per Square Kilometre.

New Zealand and the United Kingdom clearly come out on top with almost 101 and 140 respective sheep for each Sqaure Kilometre of land on their relatively small islands.

Sheep-to-Human Populations (2018)

To the right are the top five countries with most sheep to land ratios.

The only country with more sheep than humans is New Zealand, with five times the amount. In a turnabout from the previous map where the United Kingdom is shown to have the most densely packed sheep, humans are even more densely packed - there is almost double the amount of humans in the United Kingdom than there is sheep.

Sheep Locations in New Zealand (2015-2019)

Sheep are spread all over New Zealand, both on the North Island, and South Island. There is a cluster of relatively smaller sheep populaces at the top of the South Island, in the regions of Tasman, Nelson and Marlborough.

Upon filtering to looking at Lambs (Ewe Born), we can see that they are the largest counts of sheep in New Zealand.

Sheep Product Prices in New Zealand (2015-2019)

Of the many products parts of a sheep that can be harvested for sale, Lamb and Mutton are quite expensive per sheep, with lamb consistently outcosting mutton.

Meanwhile, the three types of wool shown here go for a much lower price. Most products' value took a dive from 2015-2016, but all recovered except for Strong Wool, which has continued to trend downwards in price since 2016.
